Evan Munro

I am a first-year PhD student in Economics at Stanford GSB. I recently graduated with an MPhil in Economics from Oxford University, where my thesis was on the performance of boosting and lasso under multicollinearity. Before that, I was an analyst at Goldman Sachs in a credit derivatives trading group known as Principal Funding and Investment. I completed my B.A. at Columbia University, where my research with Prof. Serena Ng focused on empirical macroeconomics and big data.

My current research is on applications of machine learning to economics, especially the use of latent variable models to estimate hidden factors that drive consumers and firms' behavior and expectations.
