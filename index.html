Evan Munro

I am a first-year PhD student in Economics at Stanford GSB. I recently graduated with an MPhil in Economics from Oxford University, where my thesis was on the performance of boosting and lasso under multicollinearity. I completed my B.A. at Columbia University, where my research with Prof. Serena Ng focused on empirical macroeconomics and big data.

My current research is on applications of machine learning to economics, especially the use of Bayesian latent variable models to estimate structural econometric models.

        

Working Papers

Hierarchical Latent Variable Models for Economic Survey Data (with Serena Ng)

Existing methods for extracting summary indexes from multivariate discrete data are generally ad-hoc, often treating discrete variables as if they were continuous. We introduce Bayesian structural models for estimating indexes from time series survey data and extract insights from the Gallup World Poll Social Series and the Michigan Survey of Consumers.

Research Projects

Deep Learning for Restaurant Choice

The state of the art methods in predicting consumer choice are Bayesian matrix factorization methods. This projects explores challenges and opportunities with using neural networks with embedding layers to predict restaurant choice from a large dataset of individual lunch choices in the Bay Area. Written in PyTorch and submitted as the final project for Stanford's Deep Learning class (CS230).
